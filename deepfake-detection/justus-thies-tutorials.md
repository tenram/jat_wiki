## Overview

A running catalog of tutorials, demos, and public-facing talks by Justus Thies (co-author of [FaceForensics++](faceforensics-plusplus.md)) spanning facial capture, neural rendering, and media-forensics education from 2016 to 2023.

## Notable Milestones

Early public demos of real-time facial reenactment (Face2Face at SIGGRAPH 2016, Nvidia GTC, and the Jimmy Kimmel Show) established the manipulation techniques that FaceForensics++ later built detection benchmarks around. Later entries shift toward policy and public education: briefings to the EU Commission and German Cabinet (2018-2019) on synthetic-media risk, an expert interview for the European Parliamentary Research Service on deepfakes (2021), and a public exhibition on facial reenactment and manipulated-media detection (2023). Academic tutorials appear at CVPR, ECCV, 3DV, and SIGGRAPH throughout.

The arc visible across the page, real-time manipulation demo → academic detection benchmark → policy briefing → public exhibition, tracks the broader deepfake research-to-policy pipeline this researcher has been part of.
